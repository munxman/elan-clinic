The Clinical Reality

What Happens When the Prescription Ends

67%

of GLP-1 patients regain two-thirds of their lost weight within one year of stopping medication. This is not a failure of willpower. It is a predictable physiological response — and one that is preventable with the right protocol in place.

GLP-1 receptor agonists work. They suppress appetite, regulate glucose, and produce measurable results. But they were never designed to be a permanent solution — and the healthcare system has no structured plan for what comes next.

When you stop, your body's hunger hormones rebound. Your metabolism, already adapted to the lower weight, resists. The signals that made it easy to eat less simply disappear. Without a maintenance protocol, the biology works against you.

◻

Your GP Has No Plan

General practitioners are trained to prescribe and monitor — not to engineer long-term metabolic maintenance. The 10-minute consultation is not designed for this level of ongoing clinical management.

◻

Telehealth Abandons You

The weight loss app or telehealth provider that got you started has no economic model for the patient who no longer needs a prescription. You become invisible to the system at exactly the moment you need it most.

◻

Willpower Is Not the Variable

The research is unambiguous: post-GLP-1 regain is driven by hormonal and neurological changes, not discipline. Treating it as a willpower problem is both inaccurate and counterproductive. The correct response is clinical, not motivational.

◻

The Window Closes Fast

The first six months after stopping or tapering GLP-1 medication are critical. Metabolic adaptation accelerates. Muscle loss, hormonal rebound, and behavioural patterns become harder to reverse the longer they go unaddressed.

Regular bloodwork, body composition assessment, and physiological markers tracked over time. We do not manage what we do not measure. Every protocol is informed by data, not assumption — quarterly at minimum, monthly where indicated.

Protocol Continuity

03

Behavioural Architecture
