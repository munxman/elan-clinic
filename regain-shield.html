Why This Happens

Your body has a set point.
GLP-1s shift it. Stopping resets it.

GLP-1 medications don't just suppress appetite ‚Äî they change how your brain processes food signals, slow gastric emptying, and reduce the hormonal response that drives hunger. While you're on them, your body cooperates. When you stop, all of that reverses.

Hunger hormones normalize. The satiety signals that the medication was providing disappear. Your brain returns to its previous state ‚Äî but often with a metabolism that's adapted to consuming less. This is why regain is so rapid. This is why "just eat less and move more" doesn't work the way people expect after stopping.
